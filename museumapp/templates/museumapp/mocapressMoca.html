The most significant objective of the museum is to conserve and preserve the unique collection of Indo Portuguese art objects, found extensively in Goa.

The various categories of museum objects not only need a great deal of expertise to conserve, but the entire process also needs to be documented in great detail for reference in the future. Some of the key objects from the Museum’s collection are also being documented for immersive technologies applications in the future. As part of the refurbishment project that commenced in 2017, the art objects from the Museum’s collection were documented and conserved by a team of conservators from INTACH, New Delhi. A temporary Conservation lab was set up at the Museum for this purpose.

The objects in the collection classified in different categories as per their material were carefully examined for any damages that were occurring on them, detailed photographs of each object were taken before, during and after the treatment, based on which the Conservators formulated the Conservation treatment for each object. The conservation treatments, which included Mechanical and Solvent cleaning, Insecticidal treatments, Removal of previous faulty restorations, Consolidation, Reintegration and Re Stabilisation; differed for each object, depending on its condition

During the course of conservation works, the INTACH conservators trained and were assisted by a team of local interns
